**Status:** Active

# Our Vision

Most software helps people **do more**.

Personal OS exists to help people **decide better**.

We believe the next generation of software will not simply execute commands or answer questions. It will understand context, remember what matters, reason across multiple domains, and provide transparent guidance while leaving the final decision to the user.

Personal OS is our attempt to build that future.

---

# Why Personal OS Exists

Today's digital life is fragmented.

Different applications manage different parts of our lives:

* Finance
* Calendar
* Health
* Notes
* Documents
* Email
* Messaging
* Tasks
* Learning
* Shopping

These applications rarely understand one another.

As a result:

* Information is scattered.
* Context is lost.
* Decisions are made with incomplete information.
* Users repeat the same information across multiple systems.
* AI assistants forget previous interactions.

Personal OS exists to unify these disconnected experiences into a single intelligent platform.

---

# The Future We Believe In

Artificial Intelligence should not replace human decision-making.

Instead, it should:

* Provide relevant context.
* Retrieve meaningful memories.
* Present trusted knowledge.
* Explain alternatives.
* Highlight risks.
* Recommend actions.
* Learn from previous decisions.

Ultimately, the user remains in control.

---

# Mission

Build an open, modular, extensible AI Operating System that enables individuals to make better decisions through:

* Long-term Memory
* Knowledge Management
* Event-Driven Architecture
* Decision Intelligence
* AI Agents
* Workflow Automation
* Explainable Recommendations

while ensuring complete ownership of personal data.

---

# Our Principles

## Human-Centered

Technology exists to improve people's lives.

Every feature should solve a real user problem.

---

## Explainability

Recommendations should always answer:

* Why?
* Why not?
* What evidence?
* What assumptions?
* What confidence?
* What alternatives?

Users should understand how decisions are reached.

---

## User Ownership

Users own:

* Their data
* Their memories
* Their documents
* Their preferences
* Their decision history

Data should be portable, exportable, and never used for advertising.

---

## Privacy by Design

Privacy is a default, not an option.

Whenever possible:

* Store data locally.
* Encrypt sensitive information.
* Minimize external dependencies.
* Support self-hosting.

---

## Open Source

Personal OS is intended to be an open-source platform.

Every architectural decision should encourage:

* Transparency
* Collaboration
* Community contributions
* Extensibility

---

## Modularity

Everything should be replaceable.

Adding new functionality should require extending the platform—not modifying its core.

---

## Simplicity

Complexity should exist inside the platform, not in the user experience.

The platform should feel intuitive even if the underlying architecture is sophisticated.

---

# What We Want to Build

Personal OS is not a single application.

It is a platform composed of independent capabilities.

Examples include:

* Personal Finance
* Health
* Calendar
* Goals
* Knowledge
* Shopping
* Travel
* Career
* Journal
* Investments

Every capability contributes to a unified understanding of the user.

---

# AI Philosophy

AI is a collaborator.

Not an authority.

AI should:

* Recommend
* Explain
* Compare
* Analyze
* Summarize
* Forecast

AI should never silently make irreversible decisions on behalf of users.

Business rules remain deterministic and transparent.

---

# Decision Intelligence

The central purpose of Personal OS is decision support.

Every recommendation should combine:

* Current context
* Historical memory
* Relevant knowledge
* User preferences
* Business rules
* Explainable reasoning

Decision quality is more important than automation.

---

# Long-Term Memory

Memory is more than conversation history.

Personal OS treats memory as structured knowledge about the user.

Examples include:

* Preferences
* Habits
* Goals
* Experiences
* Past decisions
* Important events

The platform should continuously learn while remaining transparent about what it remembers.

---

# Knowledge

Knowledge represents information about the world.

Examples:

* Documentation
* Research
* Books
* Articles
* PDFs
* Web pages

Knowledge is intentionally separated from user memory.

This distinction keeps personal context and external information independent while allowing both to contribute to better decisions.

---

# Platform Philosophy

Personal OS is designed as an operating system rather than a collection of applications.

Core responsibilities include:

* Runtime management
* Component discovery
* Capability registration
* Event routing
* Workflow execution
* Memory management
* Decision orchestration

Everything else extends the platform.

---

# Success

We consider Personal OS successful when:

* Users make better decisions.
* AI explanations are trusted.
* Data ownership remains with the user.
* New capabilities can be added without redesigning the platform.
* Contributors can extend the system with minimal friction.
* Documentation and implementation evolve together.

Success is measured by usefulness, trust, and maintainability—not by the number of AI features.
